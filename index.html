About

As of summer of '17 I am starting my 4th year in the Stanford Applied Physics PhD program working under Professor Shanhui Fan. Broadly, I do research in computational and theoretical photonics. I am part of the Accelerator on a Chip International Program , which has the goal of building miniature particle accelerators on a chip using advanced laser and nanofabrication technology (video explanation). My main research interest is in computational design with applications to photonic devices: I am developing numerical and theoretical tools to solve scientifically interesting optimization problems. In addition, I am thinking about efficient methods for supplying the optical power needed to drive these accelerators. Please see my ‘research’ tab for more details.

I am extremely interested in AI and machine learning, most notably the effects that these technologies will inevtiably have (and are having!) in society. I am currently applying the technical aspects of these fields to my research in photonics, by (1) coming up with powerful algorithms for designing optical devices and (2) thinking about light-based computational hardware platforms for performing certain operations faster and with better energy efficiency.

I grew up in San Diego and went to the University of Michigan for my undergraduate degree. While I was initially a Philosophy major, I defected halfway into my undergrad and graduated with a BS in physics. In addition to physics I spent a good amount of focus in studying complex systems. Of particular interest was social network theory, nonlinear dynamics, and emergent phenomena. At Michigan, I did research with Prof. Stephen Forrest on making economical thin-film solar cells, which gave me a lot of experience working with dangerous chemicals in the cleanroom. After that, I spent some time traveling in Asia and ended up in Singapore working on trapped ion platforms for quantum computation. Later, I worked as a junior software developer at a startup GudTech Inc. before starting my PhD.

When I’m not doing science, you can find me surfing, rock climbing, or experimenting with various fermentation projects. See my personal projects page for more details.
